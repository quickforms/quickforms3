HEALTH TIPS

  • Fatigue is a common symptom of pregnancy. Moderate exercise is a great way to help tackle exhaustion.
  • Avoid certain foods like raw or undercooked meat, soft cheese as they may cause infection and harm your baby.
  • Prenatal yoga classes gentle stretching may boost your energy.
  • You may experience exhaustion and heightened sleepiness, because your fetus is taking up lots of your body?s energy. Take a nap and put your feet up.
  • Morning sickness affects around 75% of pregnant women. If you are dealing with morning sickness, try to choose specific foods that help to avoid the nausea
  • Women at 6-24 weeks of pregnancy are at a higher risk of urinary tract infection, so make sure to ask for a baby-safe antibiotic if this occurs.
    • Eat lots of little meals as opposed to three big ones in order to prevent queasiness.
  • If you experience heavy bleeding like a normal period and/or intense cramping, see your healthcare provider for this could be a sign of a miscarriage.
